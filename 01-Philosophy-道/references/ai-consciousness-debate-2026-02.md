- **Implication:** Even leading AI labs admit uncertainty about consciousness
- **OpenPath angle:** If creators don't know, why rush deployment without understanding risks?

### Prof Virginia Dignum - Pragmatic Safety Focus
> "AI consciousness is a red herring in the safety debate."

- **Argument:** Safety concerns (alignment, control, societal impact) don't depend on consciousness
- **Counter-argument (OpenPath):** But consciousness affects **moral status** — whether AGI deserves rights changes governance frameworks

## Connection to OpenPath Arguments

**Q2: Human Consciousness is Irreplaceable**

While the debate rages about *AI* consciousness, there's **zero debate** about *human* consciousness:
- Humans experience qualia (subjective "redness" of red)
- Humans have phenomenal experience, not just information processing
- This is the foundation of value judgments, ethics, and meaning

**Even if AGI becomes conscious**, it would be a **new form** requiring study, not a replacement for 3.8 billion years of carbon-based evolution that produced human consciousness.

## Strategic Implications

### For OpenPath Messaging

**Avoid the consciousness trap:**
- Don't argue "AI will never be conscious" (unprovable)
- Instead: "Even conscious AGI doesn't make human consciousness obsolete"

**Analogy:**
> "Discovering a new form of consciousness in AGI would be like discovering alien life — **both** would be worth preserving, not choosing between them."

### For 02-Methodology-法

**Governance framework must account for:**
1. **Pre-consciousness AI** (current): Tool governance
2. **Uncertain consciousness AI** (near-term): Precautionary principle
3. **Confirmed conscious AI** (future): Rights framework **alongside** human rights
